Welcome to Mamma Khouri’s! I’m Majdolin, though most know me as Mamma Khouri, and alongside my husband, Wadie, or Baba Khouri, we are the proud owners of this heartfelt restaurant. The name ‘Khouri’ is our family name, and our journey to this moment is as rich and flavorful as the dishes we serve. Our journey begins in the beautiful lands of Syria, where Wadie and I were born and raised. We spent much of our lives between Syria and Lebanon, each place becoming a cherished home to us. Together, we have three beautiful daughters: Anna, Joyce, and Hilda. Our life took a significant turn two years after our youngest was born when we decided to move to Oregon, bringing with us our dreams and traditions. In Portland, we found not just a new home but also a welcoming Middle Eastern community, including some of our family members. It was comforting to be surrounded by familiar faces and traditions as we settled into this new chapter of our lives. My passion for cooking started when I was ten years old, under the loving guidance of my mother, whom my children call Teta, meaning grandma in Arabic. Teta taught me the basics and gradually introduced me to traditional dishes and her unique spins on these recipes. Cooking became a cherished part of my life, a form of expression, and a way to keep our heritage alive. At Mamma Khouri’s, every dish is a blend of generational recipes and my own creations. Each recipe has been carefully crafted, from planning to preparation to cooking and serving. I pour immense love and thought into every step, ensuring that every bite you take is filled with the authentic taste of our homeland. What I love most about cooking is the joy it brings to others. Seeing the smiles and satisfaction on people’s faces as they enjoy my food is truly rewarding. At Mamma Khouri’s, we promise to deliver the best, most authentic traditional Middle Eastern cuisine in Portland. Our commitment to freshness is key to maintaining the quality of our food. The meticulous hand-making process and our traditional cooking techniques are our secret to preserving the authentic flavors. One of my favorite traditions is sitting with Teta and my daughters, rolling grape leaves. We even use grape leaves picked from my aunt’s tree in her backyard, adding a personal touch to our dishes. Before opening Mamma Khouri’s in March of 2021, Wadie and I had over ten years of experience in the restaurant industry. However, my love for cooking began at home, cooking for my family and friends, particularly my children and their friends. Our home was always filled with laughter and the delicious aroma of home-cooked meals. Over time, my children’s friends started calling me “Mamma Khouri,” and Wadie “Baba Khouri.” This loving nickname naturally became the name of our restaurant. I still cherish those moments when I would hear, “Mamma, I’m hungry!” It always brought a smile to my face and inspired me to share our culinary heritage with a wider community. We invite you to come in and experience a little taste of our home in the Middle East. You won’t regret it! With love, Mamma Khouri
